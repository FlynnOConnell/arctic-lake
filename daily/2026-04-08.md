## Notes

The 2-photon PSF should be calculated as the square of the widefield PSF at the excitation wavelength (roughly 2x the emission wavelength), since two-photon excitation probability scales with intensity squared.
Before deconvolving, check sampling: the PSF core needs at least 2 pixels in every direction. If it doesn't, deconvolution isn't viable.
For noisy 2-photon data, Total Variation Richardson-Lucy is recommended over Wiener filter, though Wiener can work with careful regularization tuning.
At high laser power, the PSF physically broadens due to fluorophore saturation at the edges of the focal volume — so experimental PSFs can differ from theoretical ones depending on pulse parameters.
For thick tissue, the PSF becomes spatially variant with depth. A squared theoretical PSF works reasonably for the first few microns but degrades significantly deeper (e.g. 50µm+).
The OPTIMISME Fiji plugin was mentioned as a dedicated tool for 2-photon 3D deconvolution, worth trying as an out-of-the-box option.
