## Notes

Yesterday spent most of day debugging RBO-W1 TCC mode.

- Env vars — CUDA_VISIBLE_DEVICES/CUDA_DEVICE_ORDER cleared and verified; a stale CVD=1 was pinning to the bad card. Not the root cause.
- Driver model — fails in both TCC and WDDM (both cards set WDDM, still device_count()=1).
- Pinned alone by UUID — CVD=<2A UUID> → cudaErrorNoDevice; card is excluded at enumeration, not by filtering.
- NVML/nvidia-smi health — card fully visible: BAR1 mapped, no ECC/Xid, no retired/remapped rows, Compute Mode = Default.
- Windows — Device Manager CM_PROB_NONE, resources assigned, no conflicts.
- NVLink — nvlink -s shows both cards, 4 links each at full 14 GB/s; healthy. NVLink can't/doesn't collapse 2 GPUs to 1
- MIG — not supported on A5000; ruled out.
- Display test — the 2A card drives a monitor in WDDM, so core/VRAM/power work.
- GPU reset — nvidia-smi -i 1 -r (WDDM typically refuses).

Untested: DDU Clean Reinstall, swap cards + reseat power cables.
