## Notes

### Bead-Analyzer:

Questions bead-analyzer should answer:

- Measure how resolution varies spatially across the field (the FWHM heatmap output)
- Measure how resolution degrades with depth — beads at different Z positions will show different FWHM
- Per-camera PSF at realistic conditions for deconvolution
- QA filtering automatically rejects beads that overlap with specimen signal

Parameters:

Detection:
- box_size (default 15) — if too small, the profile extraction window clips the peak. If too large, nearby beads or specimen signal
contaminate the profile. Try adjusting based on your bead size.
- blob_min_distance — increase to avoid detecting beads near neighbors that pull the centroid off

Fitting:
- fit_window (default 20) — controls how many pixels around the peak are used for the 1D Gaussian fit. If the peak is near the edge of the
 extraction window, the fit window extends into noise/background on one side. Try reducing to 10-15.
- robust_fit=True — uses soft-L1 loss which downweights outlier pixels, can help when one side of the profile is contaminated
- local_background=True — uses annulus median instead of global min for baseline, helps when the bead sits on uneven specimen background

QA filtering:
- qa_min_symmetry — raise to 0.7-0.8. Off-center peaks produce asymmetric Z-profiles, so this filter catches exactly this problem
- qa_min_snr — raise to 5+ to reject dim beads where the peak is ambiguous
